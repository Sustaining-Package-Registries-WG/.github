# Charter of the Sustaining Package Registries Working Group

**Effective Date:** 3 March 2026

## 1. Mission

The Sustaining Package Registries Working Group (the "WG") provides a forum for repository leaders to collaborate on the financial, operational, and infrastructure challenges inherent in hosting community software at a global scale.

## 2. Scope

The WG will focus on:

- **Economic Sustainability:** Developing funding models that package registries can adopt to cover the costs of providing the services. This includes but is not limited to, infrastructure, operations and maintainers and governance.
- **Governance Enablement:** Developing shared policy frameworks and standardized terms (e.g., common terms of service and baseline contract language) to support sustainable funding.
- **Collective Messaging & Transparency:** Producing aligned communications and educational content to provide clarity and transparency to the broader ecosystem regarding sustainability efforts.

## 3. Membership and Participation

### 3.1 Restricted Membership

Participation is limited to technical and operational leaders of public-facing community software repositories. Additional stakeholders may be invited as guests by agreement of the members representing repositories.

### 3.2 Admission

Membership is subject to approval by the WG Chair(s) to verify repository leadership status.

### 3.3 Private Meetings & Open Communication

WG meetings are private and by invitation only to facilitate candid discussion. However, WG materials and findings are not confidential. Members are encouraged to share documentation and proposals with their stakeholders to build organizational support.

## 4. Governance

### 4.1 Chair

The WG shall elect a Chair or Co-Chair(s) by simple majority for a one-year term. The Chair(s) is responsible for setting and managing meeting agendas.

### 4.2 Decision Making

The WG operates by building consensus. Decisions or outputs (such as whitepapers or recommendations) represent a collective public statement from the group and are non-binding on the participating members' parent organizations or repositories.

## 5. Operating Procedures

### 5.1 Meetings

Conducted virtually or in person on a schedule determined by the membership, with advance notice where feasible.

### 5.2 Conduct

All activities and interactions shall be governed by the latest version of the Contributor Covenant.

## 6. Intellectual Property & Antitrust

### 6.1 IP Policy

The WG will not create software. Contributions to any documents produced by the WG shall be licensed under the Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International License (CC-BY-4.0).

### 6.2 Antitrust and LF Policies

All members must adhere to the Linux Foundation Antitrust Policy and other policies at https://www.linuxfoundation.org/policies. Members shall not engage in discussions regarding price-fixing, coordination of vendor pricing, vendor boycotts, or other anti-competitive behaviors as discussed in the full antitrust policy.
